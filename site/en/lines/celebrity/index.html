What's included — and what isn't

Included in your fare

  • Main dining room — Set or flexible seating
  • Buffet — Open most of the day
  • Select casual venues
  • Main theatre productions
  • Pools and sports decks
  • Fitness centre — Classes may carry a fee
  • Kids club
  • Water, drip coffee, tea, select juice

Costs extra

  • Gratuities
  • Alcohol, soda, speciality coffee — Packages available
  • Speciality restaurants — Cover charge or à la carte
  • Wi-fi — Tiered packages
  • Shore excursions
  • Spa and salon
  • Casino and arcade
  • Laundry and pressing

The facts that cost money

These are the details that quietly change your final bill and vary by fare, cabin and sailing. We verify each from its source and re-check every 30 days; anything not yet verified is shown as a gap rather than guessed.

Daily gratuitiesA published service charge added to your onboard account each day$18.00/day standard (Inside/Oceanview/Veranda); $19.00/day Concierge & AquaClass; $23.00/day The Retreat suites (per guest)
What's included vs. extraDining and entertainment vs. specialty dining, drinks, wifi, excursionsMain dining, entertainment, pools and fitness are included. The optional 'All Included' rate adds a Classic Drinks Package, Basic Wi-Fi and tips; otherwise drinks, Wi-Fi and specialty dining cost extra.
Drink package ruleWhether all adults in the cabin must buy itAll guests of legal drinking age in the same stateroom must buy the same alcoholic package (exceptions for children, teens with a zero-proof package, and pregnant/medical cases).
Wi-FiIncluded, or tiered packages at extra costTwo tiers — Basic Wi-Fi (browsing, email, messaging; included with eligible All Included rates) and Premium Wi-Fi (adds social, video calls, streaming and large files; a paid upgrade).
Deposit & final paymentWhen the balance is due before sailingGenerally 90 days before sailing; the ticket contract's no-charge cutoff actually varies by length — 75 days (1–4 nights), 90 days (5–14 nights), 120 days (15+ nights).
Cancellation & refundsPenalty schedule as the sailing gets closerBy cruise length (US ticket contract): 1–4 nights — 75+ days = no charge, 74–61 = 50%, 60–31 = 75%, 30 or fewer = 100%. 5–14 nights — 90+ = none, 89–75 = 25%, 74–61 = 50%, 60–31 = 75%, 30 or fewer = 100%. 15+ nights — 120+ = none, 119–75 = 25%, 74–61 = 50%, 60–31 = 75%, 30 or fewer = 100%.
Refundable vs non-refundableFare/deposit types and what you forfeitMost lines sell both a refundable-deposit fare and a lower non-refundable-deposit fare; a non-refundable deposit is forfeited if you cancel and change fees can apply. (General — fare types vary by sailing.)
Solo traveller supplementSingle supplement and whether solo cabins existA single supplement usually applies to solo travellers (roughly the cost of two fares); some ships offer dedicated solo cabins. Availability varies by ship and sailing — an advisor can find the solo-friendly options.
Kids — minimum age & clubsMinimum age to sail and kids-club age bandsInfants must be at least 6 months old to sail. Camp at Sea (ages 3–12): Shipmates 3–5, Cadets 6–9, Captains 10–12; separate Teen Club 13–17.
Onboard credit termsRefundable vs non-refundable onboard creditOnboard credit comes from promotions or loyalty status, not the base fare. Refundable credit can be withdrawn as cash at the end of the sailing; non-refundable credit must be spent onboard. (General — amounts and terms vary by offer.)
Loyalty programmeProgramme name and how tiers workCaptain's Club — tiers Preview, Classic, Select, Elite, Elite Plus, Zenith; points earned each sailing by stateroom category and cruise length.
Travel documentsPassport vs birth certificate on closed-loop sailingsOn closed-loop US sailings a US birth certificate plus a valid government photo ID is accepted instead of a passport (children 15 and under need only the birth certificate); a passport is preferred.

Questions people actually ask

What exactly is an infinite verandah?

A cabin design where the living space extends to the ship's edge with a retractable upper window, rather than a separate outdoor balcony. Some travellers prefer it; others miss a traditional open balcony. Understand which you're booking.

How is Celebrity different from mainstream lines?

Quieter atmosphere, stronger food and design, fewer large family attractions, and a higher fare that often includes more. Whether that trade is worth it depends on what you value on board.

Is suite class worth it?

Suite class functions almost as a separate product with its own restaurant, lounge and deck. It's a bigger step up than the equivalent on most lines.
